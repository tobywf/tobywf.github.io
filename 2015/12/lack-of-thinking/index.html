A lack of thinking

12 Dec '15

The British outrage over Trump’s latest remarks show a disturbing lack of thinking.

On one level, it shows no thought of possible different perspectives. If I was American, and the British said they hated someone, I’d not only still vote for them, I’d be more likely to vote for them - what an endorsement! (N.B.: This seems to be happening, if only I was quicker at writing these posts.)

It’s hard to see how he could have provoked more of a response. Even Mitt Romney failed to do that, by insulting the country directly. And this is why it’s particularly stupid, because Trump’s game is succ√®s de scandale 1. He’s mastered offline click-bait. Do that, and you get this brain-dead, knee-jerk socially correct response. Pavlov would be proud.

This also requires the assumption that your opinion on the matter is important. Most opinions aren’t important, let alone well informed. Who cares? (I know you don’t, I learned this the hard way via page view statistics.) If only people were so engaged in more important topics.

Finally, the petition calling for a ban is also thoughtless. Can we pause for a minute and consider how passive-aggressive petitions are? It’s like trying to institutionalise mob rule. But I digress.

Here’s one reason why. If it succeeds, you then have a diplomatic crisis on hand when the President of the USA is “banned” from your country - who incidentally you inadvertently help get elected but were trying not to. (I’m not the only person to apply a bit of logic.) Although I wouldn’t mind experiencing the number of headlines involving the words “Trump” and “card” if he got banned and elected president…
